Slide 1

Let’s Start with Chaos! 🌪️

"When Everyone Talks at Once…" 🗣️

Imagine: A classroom full of excited kids (tasks) all trying to talk to the teacher (CPU) at the same time. 🎒

Problem: Chaos! The teacher can’t listen to everyone simultaneously. 😵

Solution: Concurrency! 🚀

"The art of switching tasks so fast, it feels like magic!" ✨

Slide 2

What’s Concurrency? 🤔

"The Teacher’s Superpower" 🦸‍♀️

Kids = Tasks 👶

Processes = Kids from different classrooms (programs). 🏫

Threads = Kids from the same classroom (same program). 👯‍♂️

How it works: The teacher (CPU) switches between kids SO FAST, it looks like she’s handling everyone at once! ⚡

"It’s like spinning plates… but with kids!" 🎪

Slide 3

Time-Slicing – The Secret Sauce! ⏰

"5 Seconds Each… GO!" 🏃‍♂️

Time-Slicing: The teacher gives each kid 5 seconds to speak. ⏳

Example:

Kid 1: "I love ice cream!" 🍦

Kid 2: "Chocolate rules!" 🍫

Result: Feels like everyone’s talking together! 🎉

"The CPU’s magic trick!" 🎩

Slide 4

Context Switching – Remembering Everything! 🧠

"The Teacher’s Notebook" 📓

Before switching kids, the teacher writes down:

- What the kid was saying. 💬

- Where they left off. ⏸️

Why? So she can pick up right where they stopped! ▶️

"Like pausing a movie and resuming later!" 📝

Slide 5

Processes vs. Threads ⚔️

"Different Classes, Same Rules!" 📚

Processes: Kids from different classrooms (separate programs). 🏫

Switching takes time (new classroom = new setup). ⏱️

Threads: Kids from the same classroom (same program). 👯‍♀️

Faster switching (same toys, same rules!). ⚡

"Threads are siblings; processes are strangers!" 👫

Slide 6

Concurrency vs. Parallelism 🆚

"One Teacher vs. Many Teachers" 👩‍🏫👩‍🏫

Concurrency:

- 1 teacher (CPU) switching FAST between kids. ⚡

- Example: Single-core CPU. 💻

Parallelism:

- Many teachers (CPUs) handling kids at the SAME TIME! 👥

- Example: Multi-core CPU. 🖥️

"Concurrency = Juggling | Parallelism = Teamwork!" 🤹‍♀️👯

Slide 7

Race Conditions – Toy Fights! 🥊

"Mine! No, Mine!" 😡

Problem: Two kids (tasks) grabbing the same toy (resource). 🧸

Result: Chaos! Data corruption. 💥

Solution: Locks! 🔒

Mutex: Only ONE kid can hold the toy. 🚫

Semaphore: Limited kids (e.g., 3 at a time). 🚸

"Traffic lights for kids!" 🚦

Slide 8

Deadlocks – Stubborn Standoffs! ⚠️

"I Won’t Move Until You Do!" 😤

Example:

- Kid 1: Needs red toy → then blue toy. 🔴🔵

- Kid 2: Needs blue toy → then red toy. 🔵🔴

Result: Both stuck forever! 😫

Fix: Force one kid to give up (deadlock prevention). ✋

Slide 9

Starvation – Shy Kids Suffer! 😢

"Always the Loud Ones…" 📢

Problem: Teacher only picks loud kids (tasks). 🗣️

Solution: Fair scheduling! ⚖️

Everyone gets a turn. 🤝

"No kid left behind!" 👐

Slide 10

Scheduling – Who’s Next? 📅

"The Teacher’s Rulebook" 📜

FIFO: First kid to raise their hand speaks first. ✋

Round Robin: Equal time for everyone. ⏰

Priority: Important kids (system tasks) go first! ⭐

"Democracy vs. VIP treatment!" 🗳️👑

Slide 11

Infinite Loops – Never-Ending Stories! ♾️

"The Kid Who Won’t Stop Talking" 🗣️

Problem: One kid hogs the CPU forever. 🐷

Solution: Time quotas! ⏳

Teacher forces them to stop after a set time. ✋

"Sorry, time’s up!" ⏰
